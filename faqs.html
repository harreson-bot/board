Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about insurance plans, coverage options, and how we can help you.

What types of insurance do you offer?
I specialize in private, health-based plans, ACA/Marketplace plans, Medicare and Medicare Supplements, Life Insurance, and dental/vision. I'm licensed in 31 states and work with multiple carriers to find the best fit for your situation.
What's the difference between private health insurance and ACA marketplace plans?
ACA plans are income-based and sold on government marketplaces like healthcare.gov. Private health-based plans are not sold on public marketplaces — they offer nationwide coverage, often lower premiums, and pair well with tax strategies like the HRA-105. I can help you compare both options for your situation.
Can self-employed people really write off 100% of health insurance premiums?
Yes — and more. The IRS allows self-employed individuals to deduct 100% of health insurance premiums above-the-line (no itemizing required). With the HRA-105 structure, you can also deduct out-of-pocket medical expenses as a business deduction. Most people don't know this exists.
What is the HRA-105?
The BASE® Health Reimbursement Arrangement (Section 105 HRA) is an IRS-approved plan that lets business owners deduct health insurance premiums AND out-of-pocket medical costs as business expenses. It's been available since 1954 and was granted Safe Harbor status under the ACA. I help clients implement it correctly.
